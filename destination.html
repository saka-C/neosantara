Rice Terraces

Ubud, Balinese

Ubud is an area located in Gianyar Regency, Bali, which is famous as a center of art, culture, and tropical nature. Ubud is famous for its terraced rice fields or rice terraces, especially in Tegallalang, which combine natural beauty with the traditional Subak agricultural system, which has existed since the 9th century.

Raja Ampat

Raja Ampat, West Papua

Raja Ampat is an archipelago located in Southwest Papua Province and known as one of the richest marine areas in the world. Historically, the name Raja Ampat comes from a local legend about four kings who ruled over the four main islands: Waigeo, Misool, Salawati, and Batanta.

Bali Beaches

Bali

Bali Beach has a history closely linked to the development of Balinese tourism since the mid-20th century. Bali's beaches were originally part of the livelihoods of coastal communities who depended on the sea for their livelihood through fishing and traditional ceremonies.

Ijen Crater

Bondowoso, East Java

Ijen Crater is a volcanic complex on the border of Banyuwangi and Bondowoso, East Java, famous for its spectacular turquoise acidic sulfur lake and the rare Blue Fire phenomenon that emerges from burning sulfur gas at night, making it a unique natural destination for night hikes to see the blue fire and sunrise, as well as witnessing the activities of the local sulfur miners.

Labuan Bajo

East Nusa Tenggara

Labuan Bajo is a small town on the western tip of Flores Island, NTT, which is famous as the main gateway to Komodo National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, home to the Komodo dragon, and offers extraordinary natural beauty such as pink sand beaches and a biota-rich underwater world.

Lake Toba

North Sumatra

Lake Toba is the largest natural lake in North Sumatra, Indonesia, located in the caldera of a supervolcano. It is 100 kilometers (62 mi) long, 30 kilometers (19 mi) wide, and 508 meters (1,667 ft) deep. Located in the center of northern Sumatra, it has a surface elevation of approximately 900 meters (2,953 ft). Lake Toba is the largest lake in Indonesia and the largest volcanic lake in the world.
