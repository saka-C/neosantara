Bromo Mountain

Mount Bromo is a famous volcano in East Java, known for its beautiful landscapes and active crater.

History about bromo

Mount Bromo is one of Indonesia’s most famous volcanoes, located in East Java within the Bromo Tengger Semeru National Park. Historically, Bromo has been closely connected to the Tenggerese people, who believe the mountain is sacred and linked to the legend of Roro Anteng and Joko Seger, the ancestors of the Tengger tribe. As part of this tradition, the Yadnya Kasada ceremony is held annually, where offerings are thrown into the crater as a symbol of gratitude to the gods. What makes Mount Bromo unique is its active volcanic crater, the vast Sea of Sand surrounding it, and the breathtaking sunrise views that attract visitors from around the world. The combination of cultural heritage, dramatic landscapes, and volcanic activity makes Mount Bromo a truly iconic destination in Indonesia.

Big Mountain

Mount Bromo is unique for its active volcanic crater that can be accessed directly by visitors, which is rare among volcanoes. It is surrounded by the vast Tengger Sand Sea, creating a desert-like landscape that contrasts beautifully with the surrounding green mountains. Another distinctive feature is its spectacular sunrise views, often considered among the most beautiful in the world. In addition, Mount Bromo is deeply connected to the cultural traditions of the Tenggerese people, especially the annual Yadnya Kasada ceremony, making Bromo not only a natural attraction but also a sacred cultural site.
